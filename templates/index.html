About

Arecanut grading is crucial for ensuring quality control and market fairness, but traditional manual methods are often subjective, time-consuming, and inconsistent. To address this, our project introduces an AI-powered automated grading system built on a fine-tuned YOLOv8 model that detects and classifies arecanuts into Grade A or Grade B directly from tray images.

The system improves accuracy and scalability by using high-resolution, curated, and augmented datasets. Hyperparameters such as batch size, learning rate, and weight decay were optimized through grid search, while augmentation improved robustness. YOLOv8 was chosen for its real-time speed and accuracy, reducing manual errors and supporting market standardization.

Project Objectives:

  • Collect and preprocess diverse arecanut images to create a well-labeled dataset.
  • Develop and optimize a deep learning model to classify nuts by texture, size, and color.
  • Implement advanced image processing to enhance feature extraction and evaluation.
  • Create a user-friendly interface for farmers.

Looking ahead, we aim to expand the dataset, and scale the system for industrial applications, thus paving the way for smarter, AI-driven agriculture.
